# Product Management Curriculum

A structured, from-scratch curriculum to learn product management in real depth. Free, self-paced, built from the best practitioner resources — not academic theory.

**The goal**: make you a competent, hireable product manager who understands users, strategy, execution, and growth — without spending a dime on bootcamps.

---

## Why this exists

Most "learn product management" resources are either:
- **Too shallow** — a 2-hour overview that doesn't prepare you for real work
- **Too expensive** — bootcamps charging $2,000-$5,000 for information you can find free
- **Too academic** — case studies from 2010 that don't reflect how modern product teams operate

This curriculum solves all three. It's curated from the best free content — written by actual PMs at Google, Uber, Stripe, Airbnb — sequenced so later concepts build on earlier ones.

---

## How to use this

1. **Fork this repo** and check off boxes as you complete each item
2. **Go in order** — each phase assumes the previous one
3. **Apply what you learn** — read a framework, then practice it on a real or hypothetical product
4. **Total time**: ~6-12 months at 10-15 hours/week

## Phase 0 — PM Foundations

**Goal**: Understand what product management actually is, what PMs do day-to-day, and whether the role is right for you.

- [ ] **Read: [What is Product Management? (Product School)](https://productschool.com/blog/product-management-2/what-is-product-management/)** — 10 min. High-level overview of the role, responsibilities, and career paths.
- [ ] **Watch: [Day in the Life of a PM (Google)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TIow7MGaBSE)** — 15 min. See what a typical day actually looks like at a tech company.
- [ ] **Read: [Product Manager vs Product Owner vs Project Manager](https://www.productplan.com/learn/product-manager-vs-product-owner/)** — 10 min. These get confused constantly. Know the difference.
- [ ] **Read: [The Product Lifecycle (Pendo)](https://www.pendo.io/resources/product-lifecycle/)** — 15 min. The four phases: introduction, growth, maturity, decline. Every product goes through them.
- [ ] **Take: [Great Learning — Free Product Management Course](https://www.mygreatlearning.com/academy/learn-for-free/courses/product-management)** — 3.75 hrs. Covers PLC, marketing mix, product strategy, and the PM role. Solid foundational overview with a certificate.
- [ ] **Take: [Simplilearn SkillUp — Introduction to Product Management](https://www.simplilearn.com/introduction-to-product-management-free-course-skillup)** — 6-8 hrs. Free course with downloadable certificate. Covers product lifecycle, feature prioritization, roadmap planning, and agile basics.
- [ ] **Take: [Pendo — Product Management Basics Certification](https://www.pendo.io/product-management-basics-certification/)** — 2.5 hrs. 8 modules covering the full PM lifecycle. Free certification badge for LinkedIn. Good structured intro.
- [ ] **Read: [Aakash Gupta — PM 101: Product Management Foundations](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/complete-course-in-pm)** — 2 hrs. The first course in the free PM MBA. Covers what PMs actually do, PRDs, product reviews, and team structure.
- [ ] **Read: [The Product Manager's Role in an Agile Team (SVPG)](https://www.svpg.com/product-manager-role/)** — 10 min. Marty Cagan on the difference between "feature teams" and "empowered product teams."

**Books for this phase**: None required. If you want an intro, read *Inspired* (Marty Cagan) — but save it for Phase 1 where the concepts will land better.

## Phase 1 — User Understanding & Discovery

**Goal**: Learn how to identify real customer problems, validate them before building, and build a habit of continuous discovery.

- [ ] **Read: [The Mom Test (Rob Fitzpatrick)](https://www.momtestbook.com/)** — 3 hrs. The single most important book on customer conversations. How to ask questions that get truth, not flattery. Read the whole thing — it's short and every page matters.
- [ ] **Watch: [How to Do User Research (NN Group)](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/user-research-methods/)** — 30 min. Overview of user research methods: interviews, surveys, usability testing, field studies.
- [ ] **Read: [Jobs to Be Done: Introduction (JTBD)](https://jtbd.info/)** — 1 hr. The framework that says "people don't buy products, they hire them to do a job." Read the core articles on this site.
- [ ] **Read: [Continuous Discovery Habits (Teresa Torres)](https://www.producttalk.org/continuous-discovery-habits/)** — 4 hrs. The modern playbook for discovery. Covers opportunity solution trees, interviewing techniques, and structured mapping.
- [ ] **Read: [Aakash Gupta — PM 301: Product Discovery](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/complete-course-in-pm)** — 2 hrs. Deep dive into how top PMs run discovery — user research, experimentation, opportunity sizing.
- [ ] **Read: [How to Run a Customer Interview (Reforge)](https://www.reforge.com/blog/customer-interviews)** — 20 min. Practical guide: how to structure interviews, what questions to ask, how to synthesize findings.
- [ ] **Practice**: Interview 3-5 people about a problem they face. Write up a synthesis of what you learned. Try the "5 Whys" technique on each problem.
- [ ] **Practice**: Build an Opportunity Solution Tree for a product you use. Identify the desired outcome, the opportunities, the solutions, and experiments.

**Books for this phase**:
- *The Mom Test* by Rob Fitzpatrick (free online)
- *Continuous Discovery Habits* by Teresa Torres
- *The Lean Startup* by Eric Ries

## Phase 2 — Product Strategy

**Goal**: Learn how to set direction — vision, strategy, OKRs, roadmaps. Strategy is what separates feature-stackers from real PMs.

- [ ] **Read: [Good Strategy Bad Strategy (Richard Rumelt)](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/11721966-good-strategy-bad-strategy)** — 6 hrs. The best strategy book ever written, period. Not PM-specific but directly applicable. The kernel: diagnosis, guiding policy, coherent action.
- [ ] **Watch: [How to Write a Product Strategy (Aakash Gupta)](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/complete-course-in-pm)** — 2 hrs. PM 201: Advanced Product Strategy. Practical frameworks for writing a product strategy in 1 day / 1 week / 1 month.
- [ ] **Read: [Product Strategy vs Product Roadmap (ProductPlan)](https://www.productplan.com/learn/product-strategy-vs-roadmap/)** — 10 min. Understand the difference. Strategy = why and what; roadmap = when and how.
- [ ] **Read: [How to Set OKRs (Google)](https://www.whatmatters.com/resources/google-okr-playbook/)** — 30 min. Google's official OKR playbook. Objectives (aspirational) + Key Results (measurable). Learn the difference between committed and aspirational OKRs.
- [ ] **Read: [North Star Metric (Amplitude)](https://amplitude.com/blog/north-star-metric)** — 15 min. The one metric that captures the core value your product delivers. Examples: Airbnb = nights booked, Spotify = time listened.
- [ ] **Read: [Feature vs Outcome Roadmaps (ProductPlan)](https://www.productplan.com/learn/outcome-driven-roadmaps/)** — 10 min. Stop shipping features. Start shipping outcomes.
- [ ] **Practice**: Write a one-page product strategy for a product you use. Include: vision, target users, core problem, strategic bets, what you won't build.
- [ ] **Practice**: Set 3 OKRs for yourself or a product you know. Make them measurable. Track them for a month.

**Books for this phase**:
- *Good Strategy Bad Strategy* by Richard Rumelt
- *Measure What Matters* by John Doerr (OKRs)

## Phase 3 — Prioritization & Decision-Making

**Goal**: Master the frameworks that help you decide what to build and what to say no to.

- [ ] **Read: [RICE Prioritization Framework (Intercom)](https://www.intercom.com/blog/rice-simple-prioritization-for-product-managers/)** — 15 min. The most popular scoring framework: Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort.
- [ ] **Read: [MoSCoW Method (ProductPlan)](https://www.productplan.com/glossary/moscow-prioritization/)** — 10 min. Must-have, Should-have, Could-have, Won't-have. Simple and effective for stakeholder alignment.
- [ ] **Read: [Kano Model (NN Group)](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/kano-model/)** — 20 min. Classifies features into: basic (expected), performance (deliberate trade-off), delighters (unexpected joy).
- [ ] **Read: [Opportunity Score (Rachitsky)](https://www.lennyrachitsky.com/p/sizing-your-opportunity)** — 15 min. A qualitative scoring method based on user research. Score = importance × satisfaction gap.
- [ ] **Read: [How to Say No as a PM (Aakash Gupta)](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/complete-course-in-pm)** — 30 min. The political and strategic skill of killing features others want.
- [ ] **Read: [ICE Scoring Model (Sean Ellis)](https://www.productplan.com/glossary/ice-score/)** — 10 min. Impact, Confidence, Ease. Faster than RICE for early-stage decisions.
- [ ] **Practice**: Take 10 features from a real product. Score them with RICE. Stack-rank them. Compare your ranking to what the company actually built.
- [ ] **Practice**: Map 5 features onto the Kano model. Which are basics? Which are delighters?

**Books for this phase**:
- *Escaping the Build Trap* by Melissa Perri (the best book on why prioritization matters strategically)
- *Inspired* by Marty Cagan (has excellent chapters on deciding what to build)

## Phase 4 — Delivery & Agile Execution

**Goal**: Understand how PMs work with engineering and design to ship products — agile ceremonies, writing effective specs, managing stakeholders.

- [ ] **Read: [The Scrum Guide (2020)](https://scrumguides.org/scrum-guide.html)** — 30 min. The official Scrum definition. Read this once, then never read a Scrum blog post again. Primary source.
- [ ] **Read: [Product Requirements Documents: A Modern Guide (Aakash Gupta)](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/complete-course-in-pm)** — 45 min. How to write PRDs that engineers can actually build from.
- [ ] **Read: [User Stories (Mountain Goat Software)](https://www.mountaingoatsoftware.com/agile/user-stories)** — 15 min. The 3 C's: Card, Conversation, Confirmation. INVEST criteria.
- [ ] **Watch: [How PMs Work with Engineers (Lenny's Podcast)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pGLrCz0G6Aw)** — 1 hr. Real advice from PMs at top companies on engineering collaboration.
- [ ] **Read: [The Product Review Bible (Aakash Gupta)](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/complete-course-in-pm)** — 30 min. How to crush the scariest meeting in product — the executive product review.
- [ ] **Read: [Stakeholder Management (Mind the Product)](https://www.mindtheproduct.com/stakeholder-management-for-product-managers/)** — 15 min. Managing up, across, and down.
- [ ] **Read: [How to Run Effective Sprint Reviews (Parabol)](https://www.parabol.co/blog/sprint-review/)** — 10 min. The most commonly wasted agile ceremony. Fix it.
- [ ] **Practice**: Write a PRD for a small feature (e.g., "add dark mode to an app"). Include: problem, users, requirements, success metrics, scope boundaries.
- [ ] **Practice**: Go through a full sprint cycle with a team (or simulate it): backlog grooming → sprint planning → daily standup → sprint review → retrospective.

**Books for this phase**:
- *Inspired* by Marty Cagan (Part IV: Process)
- *Escaping the Build Trap* by Melissa Perri (Part IV: Process)

## Phase 5 — Growth, Metrics & Analytics

**Goal**: Become data-informed — not data-driven, not data-blind. Learn which metrics matter, how to run experiments, and how to grow a product.

- [ ] **Read: [Lean Analytics (Croll & Yoskovitz)](https://leananalyticsbook.com/)** — 4 hrs. The best book on choosing the right metric for your stage. One metric that matters (OMTM).
- [ ] **Read: [The AARRR Pirate Funnel (Dave McClure)](https://www.productplan.com/glossary/aarrr-framework/)** — 10 min. Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Revenue, Referral. The classic growth framework.
- [ ] **Read: [HEART Framework (Google)](https://www.mgroup.com/articles/heart-framework/)** — 20 min. Google's UX measurement framework: Happiness, Engagement, Adoption, Retention, Task Success.
- [ ] **Read: [A/B Testing Guide (Optimizely)](https://www.optimizely.com/optimization-glossary/ab-testing/)** — 30 min. Statistical significance, sample size, running clean experiments.
- [ ] **Read: [Aakash Gupta — Growth 301: Growth Fundamentals](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/complete-course-in-pm)** — 2 hrs. Core growth concepts: activation, retention loops, viral coefficients, growth models.
- [ ] **Read: [Aakash Gupta — Growth 401: Growth Systems](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/complete-course-in-pm)** — 2 hrs. Advanced growth: growth models, North Star frameworks, experimentation systems.
- [ ] **Read: [Product-Led Growth (OpenView)](https://openviewpartners.com/product-led-growth/)** — 1 hr. PLG Playbook. How products like Slack, Zoom, and Calendly grow through the product itself.
- [ ] **Read: [Cohort Analysis (Mixpanel)](https://mixpanel.com/blog/cohort-analysis-definition/)** — 15 min. Tracking groups of users over time. The most actionable type of product analytics.
- [ ] **Practice**: Define 3 metrics for a product. Identify the North Star, one input metric, and one vanity metric to ignore.
- [ ] **Practice**: Design a fake A/B test: hypothesis, variant, sample size, minimum detectable effect, success metric. Write the analysis you'd do.

**Books for this phase**:
- *Lean Analytics* by Croll & Yoskovitz
- *Hooked* by Nir Eyal (habit formation)
- *Product-Led Growth* by Wes Bush

## Phase 6 — Specializations

**Goal**: Dive deeper into one or two areas depending on your career interests.

### Track A: B2B / Enterprise & Pricing

- [ ] **Read: [Aakash Gupta — B2B 401: B2B Product & Pricing](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/complete-course-in-pm)** — 2 hrs. SaaS pricing, packaging, go-to-market for B2B products.
- [ ] **Read: [SaaS Pricing Strategy (Chargebee)](https://www.chargebee.com/resources/saas-pricing-strategy/)** — 45 min. Per-seat, usage-based, tiered, value-based. When to use which.
- [ ] **Read: [Enterprise Sales for PMs (SVPG)](https://www.svpg.com/product-managers-and-enterprise-sales/)** — 15 min. How PMs support enterprise deals without becoming salespeople.
- [ ] **Read: [The Complete Guide to B2B Product Management (ProductPlan)](https://www.productplan.com/learn/b2b-product-management/)** — 30 min. Differences from B2C: longer sales cycles, multiple buyers, contracts, compliance.

### Track B: AI Product Management

- [ ] **Read: [Aakash Gupta — AI PM Foundations](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/ai-pm-learning-roadmap)** — 2 hrs. Covers AI PM 101-405: AI foundations, strategy, tools, RAG, agents, evals, UX, career.
- [ ] **Read: [Building AI-Powered Products (Harvard Business Review)](https://hbr.org/2024/07/building-ai-powered-products)** — 30 min. Practical framework for productizing AI capabilities.
- [ ] **Read: [The AI Product Manager's Handbook (Andrew Ng)](https://www.deeplearning.ai/)** — 4 hrs. Andrew Ng's course on how to scope, build, and ship AI products.
- [ ] **Read: [Product Management for AI (Product School)](https://productschool.com/blog/artificial-intelligence/product-management-for-ai/)** — 20 min. How AI changes discovery, evaluation, and iteration.
- [ ] **Practice**: Design an AI feature. Start with the user problem, not the technology. Define: success criteria, data requirements, failure modes, evaluation protocol.

### Track C: Product Design

- [ ] **Read: [The Design of Everyday Things (Don Norman)](https://www.nngroup.com/books/design-of-everyday-things/)** — 6 hrs. The foundational book on UX design. Affordances, signifiers, feedback, conceptual models.
- [ ] **Read: [Don't Make Me Think (Steve Krug)](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/18197267-don-t-make-me-think-revisited)** — 3 hrs. Web/usability design. Short, practical, hilarious.
- [ ] **Read: [Aakash Gupta — Design 401: Product Design](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/complete-course-in-pm)** — 2 hrs. How PMs and designers collaborate, design thinking, wireframing.
- [ ] **Read: [The PM's Guide to Wireframes (Aakash Gupta)](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/complete-course-in-pm)** — 30 min. Practical wireframing for non-designers. Low-fi, mid-fi, hi-fi.
- [ ] **Practice**: Redesign the login flow of an app you use. Sketch wireframes (paper or Figma). Explain your decisions.

## Phase 7 — Interview & Career Prep

**Goal**: Convert knowledge into a job offer.

- [ ] **Read: [Cracking the PM Interview (Gayle McDowell)](https://www.crackingthepminterview.com/)** — 6 hrs. The standard PM interview prep book. Covers resume, behavioral, product design, strategy, and estimation questions.
- [ ] **Read: [Aakash Gupta — Career 301: Interview Mastery](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/complete-course-in-pm)** — 2 hrs. How to answer any PM interview question. Frameworks for product design, strategy, analytics, and behavioral rounds.
- [ ] **Read: [Aakash Gupta — Career 401: Job Search Systems](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/complete-course-in-pm)** — 2 hrs. How to run a structured job search: targeting, networking, referrals, applications.
- [ ] **Read: [Aakash Gupta — Career 402: Big Tech & High Comp](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/complete-course-in-pm)** — 2 hrs. How to get into FAANG-level PM roles. What they look for, how to negotiate.
- [ ] **Practice**: Answer 10 PM interview questions out loud. Record yourself. Refine.
  - "Design a vending machine for the blind."
  - "How would you improve Instagram?"
  - "Estimate the number of gas stations in the US."
  - "Our retention dropped 10% — how do you investigate?"
- [ ] **Practice**: Build a portfolio case study. Pick a product you improved. Document: problem, process, outcome, your specific contribution.
- [ ] **Read: [The PM Resume (Lenny's Newsletter)](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/how-to-write-a-great-product-manager)** — 15 min. Quantified achievements, not job descriptions.

## Appendix A — Key Frameworks (Quick Reference)

| Framework | What It Is | When To Use |
|---|---|---|
| **RICE** | Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort scored 1-10 | Prioritizing features with data |
| **ICE** | Impact, Confidence, Ease (simpler than RICE) | Early-stage / growth experiments |
| **MoSCoW** | Must/Should/Could/Won't | Alignment with stakeholders |
| **Kano Model** | Basic / Performance / Delighter | Feature categorization |
| **OKRs** | Objective + 3-5 Key Results | Setting quarterly goals |
| **North Star** | One metric that captures core value | Strategic alignment |
| **JTBD** | Jobs to be Done framework | User research & discovery |
| **Opportunity Solution Tree** | Outcome → Opportunities → Solutions → Experiments | Structuring discovery |
| **AARRR (Pirate Metrics)** | Acquisition → Activation → Retention → Revenue → Referral | Growth analysis |
| **HEART** | Happiness, Engagement, Adoption, Retention, Task Success | UX measurement |
| **Design Thinking** | Empathize → Define → Ideate → Prototype → Test | Problem solving |
| **Double Diamond** | Discover → Define → Develop → Deliver | Design process |
